# RoamVault Design Context

## Users

**Who:** Obsidian power users who plan trips using structured vaults with templates and frontmatter conventions. Technical, detail-oriented people who value organization.

**Context:** Planning travel — creating trip files, adding activities, day plans, and stops. Used before and during trip planning, likely at a desk or on a laptop.

**Job to be done:** Quickly create properly structured travel notes without the friction of manually creating files, applying templates, and filling frontmatter in Obsidian.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Minimal, precise, functional

**Emotional goal:** Calm and organized. The interface should feel like a well-organized drawer — everything in its place, no clutter, peaceful control over logistics.

**Voice:** Quiet confidence. The tool does its job without drawing attention to itself. No excitement, no flair — just reliable utility.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Warm minimalism. Retain the earthy, journal-like color palette (browns, creams, warm tones) but strip away decorative elements. The warmth comes from color, not from effects or ornamentation.

**Theme:** Light mode primary, with dark mode support following system preference.

**What to keep:**

- Warm earthy palette (browns, creams, muted greens/blues for badges)
- Serif headings for a touch of personality
- Clean card layouts with trip banner images

**What to reduce:**

- Background textures and gradients
- Backdrop blur effects
- Visual noise and decoration

**Anti-references:**

- Generic SaaS dashboards with too many cards and metrics
- Overly playful travel apps with illustrations everywhere
- AI-generated designs with gradient text, glowing borders, side-stripes

## Design Principles

1. **Quiet utility** — The interface should feel invisible when working. No element should call attention to itself unless it's actionable.

2. **Warmth through restraint** — Use the earthy palette deliberately. A single warm accent is more powerful than warmth everywhere.

3. **Precision in spacing** — Every margin, padding, and gap should feel intentional. Consistent rhythm creates calm.

4. **Content-first hierarchy** — Trip names, dates, and banners are the content. UI chrome should recede.

5. **Respect the vault** — RoamVault is a window into an Obsidian vault, not a replacement. Design should feel complementary to Obsidian's aesthetic.
